Battery Storage Arbitragei100 MW / 4-hour battery ($120M capex, 85% RTE). Each day: charge during the 4 cheapest hours, discharge during the 4 most expensive. Revenue = (discharge × RTE − charge) × capacity.

⚡ How This Dashboard Works

This tool runs a 7-stage analytics pipeline across all 7 US electricity markets (ISOs/RTOs). Each stage transforms raw data into structured outputs powering every chart, KPI, and table. Select any ISO tab to switch markets. Use the nav to explore each analytical layer.

📥 Stage 1 — Ingest

Pulls raw load, price, weather, and queue data. Supports live API sources or sample CSVs. Validates against schema contracts and logs a signed manifest with SHA-256 checksums for data lineage.

🔄 Stage 2 — Transform

Cleans and joins the four raw datasets into a single hourly panel. Normalizes the queue table with standardized project fields and completion probabilities.

📊 Stage 3 — Forecast

Builds a long-range load forecast using a naive 24h lag baseline and weather-adjusted regression. Backtests both, selects the best, then projects 10 years under Low / Base / High growth scenarios.

📌 Stage 4 — Queue Model

Applies completion probability weights to each queue project. P50 (base) and P90 (conservative) scenarios estimate how much capacity will actually come online each year by technology.

⚡ Stage 5 — Markets

Computes wholesale market analytics: solar and wind capture prices, negative price hours, congestion proxy spread, and the hourly emissions intensity model.

💵 Stage 6 — Finance

Full project finance model for a representative utility-scale solar plant. Runs 18 scenarios across contract type × price case × capex case. Outputs NPV, IRR, DSCR, and LCOE for each. Also models battery storage arbitrage.

Key Terms

Plain-English definitions for every metric in the dashboard

📈 Market Terms

  • LMP — Locational Marginal Price. Wholesale electricity price at a node, in $/MWh.
  • Capture Price — What a solar/wind plant actually earns, weighted by when it generates. Usually below avg hub price due to cannibalization.
  • Capture Ratio — Capture price ÷ avg hub price. Below 1.0 = earns less than average.
  • Price Duration Curve — All hours sorted high to low. Shows how often prices exceed any given level.
  • Negative Prices — When price < $0. Generators pay to produce. Caused by excess must-run generation (wind, nuclear).
  • Congestion — Price differences from transmission limits. High congestion = grid bottlenecks.

💹 Finance Terms

  • NPV — Net Present Value. Total value in today's dollars. Positive = project creates value above required return.
  • IRR — Internal Rate of Return. Discount rate at which NPV = 0. Compare to your hurdle rate.
  • DSCR — Debt Service Coverage Ratio. EBITDA ÷ Debt Service. Must stay ≥ 1.0×; lenders require ≥ 1.25×.
  • LCOE — Levelized Cost of Energy. All-in cost per MWh over project life. Break-even price.
  • PPA — Power Purchase Agreement. Long-term fixed price contract that removes merchant price risk.
  • RTE — Round-Trip Efficiency. Energy out ÷ energy in for a battery. 85% = 15% energy lost in storage cycle.

📌 Queue Terms

  • Interconnection Queue — List of projects that applied to connect to the grid. ISO must study each project's grid impact before approving.
  • COD — Commercial Operation Date. Target date a project starts generating.
  • P50 / P90 — Probability scenarios. P50 = base case. P90 = conservative (fewer projects complete).
  • Queue Attrition — 60–80% of queued MW historically never gets built.

🌎 The 7 US ISOs / RTOs

  • ERCOT — Texas. Isolated grid, high volatility, rapid wind/solar growth.
  • CAISO — California. High solar, duck curve, frequent negative midday prices.
  • PJM — Mid-Atlantic + Midwest. Largest ISO by load. Strong capacity market.
  • MISO — Midwest + South. Wind-dominant, lowest average prices.
  • SPP — Great Plains. Highest wind penetration in the US.
  • NYISO — New York. Premium NYC pricing, offshore wind buildout.
  • ISO-NE — New England. Smallest ISO, gas-dependent, aggressive offshore wind.

Data & Limitations

📋 Data Sources

Uses stylized sample data with realistic market parameters per ISO. The pipeline is architected to connect to live ERCOT APIs, Open-Meteo weather archive, and ISO queue filings when configured with real endpoints in the config YAML files.

⚠ Model Limitations

  • Congestion is a proxy spread, not a power-flow model
  • Capture prices use stylized generation profiles
  • Finance model = single project, not a portfolio
  • Emissions = proxy model scaled by price signal
  • Queue completion = rule-based, not ML-calibrated

🔧 Running the Pipeline

Click Pipeline in the nav to trigger a fresh data run. Use the Custom Assumptions sliders in Finance to explore scenarios live. Set up a daily cron job via the Pipeline tab to keep data current automatically.
